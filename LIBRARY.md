### Art Games segment

### match me

_Art Games · physical game_

> each child has a 9-grid template. pick a bead from the bowl without looking. if it matches a square on your grid, place it.

**How to play**

1. Coloured beads sit in a bowl or box in the centre.
2. On their turn, a child picks one bead without looking, compares it to their grid, and places it if it matches.
3. No match = bead goes back.
4. First to finish a row or grid wins.

**Variations**

- **Variation 1 — Picture matching** — Child draws a picture card from a deck and places it on the matching colour grid. If no match, discard. First to finish a row or grid wins.
- **Variation 2 — Pattern Bingo** — Teacher calls out a colour + pattern combination (e.g. "yellow straight lines"). Children mark if present. First to finish a row or grid wins.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** match colours only — with beads or picture cards.
- **Medium:** Pattern Bingo — teacher calls out a colour + pattern combination and children mark if present.
- **Hard:** children play Bingo without teacher's cues — each child takes a turn to call out a colour + pattern.

**Materials**

- 9-grid template card
- Coloured beads in a bowl or box
- Coloured object cards
- Timer
- Dry-erase markers (optional)


### colour flip

_Art Games · physical game_

> 56 colour cards face-down. flip two. matching colour = keep the pair. most pairs wins.

**How to play**

1. Shuffle cards and place them face down.
2. Children take turns flipping two cards on each turn.

**Variations**

- **Variation 1 — Colour matching** — Each player flips two Colour Cards on their turn. If both cards are the same colour, they keep the pair. If not, they flip the cards back. The player with the most pairs at the end wins.
- **Variation 2 — Colour Mixing cards** — Each player starts with an Easy Mixing Card. On their turn, flip two Colour Cards from the grid. If the flipped colours match the formula on the Mixing Card, they keep the cards — if not, they place them back.
- **Variation 3 — Missing mixing card** — The teacher gives a Medium or Difficult Mixing Card with either 1 or 2 colours missing. Children find the matching pair by flipping 2 cards.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Colour matching only.
- **Medium:** Easy and Medium Mixing Cards.
- **Hard:** Difficult Mixing Cards. Timer can be used to limit time.

**Materials**

- 56 Colour Cards
- Easy Mixing Cards (14)
- Medium Mixing Cards (14)
- Difficult Mixing Cards (28)


### shape stitch

_Art Games · physical game_

> sew through templates using shoelaces. running stitch, backstitch, or a pattern combining stitches of your choice.

**How to play**

1. Children sew through templates using shoelaces.
2. Running Stitch = dashed line.
3. Backstitch = continuous joined line.
4. Whip stitch = over the edges in a loop.
5. Pattern Stitching = combines stitch types in a design of the child's choosing.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Running Stitch and Whip stitch.
- **Medium:** Backstitch.
- **Hard:** Pattern stitching.

**Materials**

- Sewing templates (numbers, alphabets, and object outlines)
- Shoelaces


### shape fusion

_Art Games · physical game_

> build a subject from geometric pieces without speaking. your team guesses what you are making.

**How to play**

1. Each child builds a subject from geometric pieces silently while their team guesses what it is.

**Variations**

- **Variation 1 — Child's choice** — The child picks which subject they want to create from the Shape Card and the other children guess.
- **Variation 2 — Relay / race** — The group splits into teams and the teams compete to make the most subjects — whichever team finishes a card, or a set number of cards, first wins.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** B1 cards, educator names category.
- **Medium:** B1 or B2, no hints.
- **Hard:** B2 cards, relay format.

**Materials**

- Two sets of 60 transparent geometric pieces
- Shape Cards B1 and B2

### artventure

### imagine that

_Art Games · physical game_

> one child describes a card image without naming it. others draw what they hear.

**How to play**

1. One child is the describer.
2. The describer describes the card without naming the subject.
3. The other children draw what they hear.
4. After everyone is done, the describer picks the drawing that best matches what they had in mind.
5. Variation — the child invents an imaginary subject and describes it while drawing simultaneously — for example, a whale with the body of a horse.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** easy cards — the guessing audience can ask the describer 2 questions.
- **Medium:** easy cards — no questions allowed.
- **Hard:** difficult cards — no clues allowed.

**Materials**

- Physical card decks — Animals and Objects
- Drawing paper
- Colour pencils or markers


### doodle dash

_Art Games · physical game_

> teacher reads a prompt. everyone draws. unique drawings score a point, duplicates score zero.

**How to play**

1. The teacher reads out the prompt to draw.
2. All children draw simultaneously for a set time, then reveal.
3. Example prompt — "something that starts with the letter C." If two or more children draw the same thing, they each score 0; a unique drawing scores a point.
4. Bonus points for the funniest or most artistic drawing, voted by the group.
5. Variation — children draw individually or as a team taking turns.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** no time limit — take as long as you need.
- **Medium:** a set time per prompt — draw before the timer runs out.
- **Hard:** rapid sketching — a set number of prompts in a limited time, team or individual. must complete every drawing before time is up.

**Materials**

- Drawing prompts — https://wordwall.net/resource/86438051/sketch-it-up-deck-2
- Drawing paper or whiteboards
- Pencils or markers
- Timer

### i spot tool/medium

- Pencils, crayons, and the identified tools/mediums

### shape fusion

_Art Games · physical game_

> build with transparent geometric pieces — either silently for your team to guess, or from a teacher cue, then sketch it capturing proportions and negative space.

**How to play**

1. Two ways to play — see variations.

**Variations**

- **Variation 1 — Build and guess** — Each child builds a subject from geometric pieces silently while their team guesses what it is. Child's choice: the child picks which subject they want to create from the Shape Card and the other children guess. Relay / race: the group splits into teams and the teams compete to make the most subjects — whichever team finishes a card, or a set number of cards, first wins.
- **Variation 2 — Composition from cues** — Each child builds a composition from transparent geometric pieces based on teacher cues (for example: teacher says "build a composition with one dominant shape supported by two smaller ones"). After building, sketch the arrangement on paper capturing proportions and negative space. Compare sketch to tile arrangement.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Variation 1 — build and guess.
- **Medium:** Variation 2 — standard gameplay.
- **Hard:** The opposite team chooses specific geometric shapes to be used, or gives cues instead of the teacher.

**Materials**

- Two sets of 60 transparent geometric pieces
- Shape Cards B1 and B2

### sentence chain

_Roll Call · facilitated · card: Brain_

### every body says

_Roll Call · physical game · card: Body_

> I'm going to show a card. Your team has to become that object — using only your bodies. No speaking.

**How to play**

1. Children divide into teams of 3–4.
2. One child flips an object image card — visible to all teams.
3. All teams immediately form that object using their bodies.
4. No speaking.
5. Teams work together silently.
6. The teacher decides which team's version is most convincing.
7. That team flips the next card.

### voice toss

_Roll Call · physical game · card: Voice_

> Before you throw the ball, call a volume. The catcher has to say any word at exactly that volume.

**How to play**

1. Children stand or sit in a circle.
2. Before throwing a soft ball, the thrower calls one of three volume levels.
3. The catcher must say any word at exactly that volume before throwing to someone new and calling a different level.
4. Three volume levels: Loud — full voice, fill the room
5. Medium — normal conversation voice
6. Whisper — barely audible, lean in to hear.

**Variations**

- **Sing / Laugh / Cry and Speak** — Before the throw, the thrower calls one of three modes — sing, laugh, or cry. The catcher says any word in that mode: sung to any tune, while laughing, or as if about to cry.
- **Volume Toss Circle** — The circle gradually moves up or down the volume scale — each catcher speaks one notch louder or one notch quieter than the last. After every 3 children, the teacher resets the cycle and the circle starts again from the beginning of the scale.

**Materials**

- Soft ball

### what's that sound

_Roll Call · facilitated · card: Ears_

### body talk

_Playground · physical game_

> Children complete a set — 4 expressions and 4 gestures — by drawing cards and performing each one in a sentence.

**How to play**

1. Each child draws a card from the expression / gesture deck.
2. The child then chooses to keep or discard the card — if they keep it, they incorporate that expression or gesture into a sentence they speak; if not, they discard it and wait for their next turn.
3. The group watches and calls out the feeling or meaning they see.
4. The round continues until every child has completed their set — 4 expressions and 4 gestures each.

**Variations**

- **Individual play** — Each child completes their own set of 4 expressions + 4 gestures on their own turns.
- **Team play** — Children play in teams. For each card, every member of the team says a different line using that same gesture or expression — a group performance of the same feeling.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Child says 1 sentence using the expression or gesture on the card.
- **Medium:** Child says 3–4 sentences using the expression or gesture on the card.
- **Hard:** The other group or person changes the topic mid-sentence and the player speaks accordingly — incorporating the new topic into the same gesture or expression.

**Materials**

- Body Talk expression and gesture card deck


### watch your step

_Playground · physical game_

> Speak on your topic for a set time to move a set number of steps. Hesitate or stop, and the timer stops — you move by however many steps the time board gives you.

**How to play**

1. Pieces start at the bottom of the gameboard.
2. The time board at the side maps speaking time to steps — e.g. 15 seconds = 1 step, 30s = 2, 45s = 3, 60s = 4, and so on.
3. Players can see the board so they can plan how long they want to keep speaking.
4. Teacher reads out a prompt from the digital deck in the app.
5. Player starts speaking on that prompt.
6. The timer runs.
7. Hesitation, repetition, or going off-topic stops the timer — the piece moves by the number of steps the time board allows for that clock reading.
8. Landing on a Slippery Step moves the piece back.
9. Landing on a Risk Step: player attempts a Risk Card challenge — completing it earns a Reward Card.
10. First individual / team to the top of the board wins.

**Variations**

- **Individual play** — Each player plays with their own piece. Their turn, their topic, their time.
- **Team play** — Teams share one piece on the board but turns are taken by team members — each member speaking on a different topic. Every member must take at least one turn before the team piece reaches the top.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Timer visible to the speaker.
- **Medium:** Timer not visible to the speaker.
- **Hard:** Use of 3-word incorporation — the opposing player gives the speaker 3 words to incorporate into their speech before the turn begins.

**Materials**

- Gameboard
- Time board (speaking-time to steps reference)
- Player pieces
- Risk Cards
- Reward Cards
- Digital prompts — https://wordwall.net/resource/84074464
- Digital prompts — https://wordwall.net/resource/84114001
- Timer

### psychiatrist

_Playground · facilitated_

> Questioner steps outside. Group agrees on a hidden rule. Questioner returns and asks questions to figure out the rule.

**How to play**

1. Questioner steps outside or turns away.
2. Group secretly agrees on a hidden rule governing all their answers — for example, everyone ends every answer with the same word, or everyone answers as if they are the person sitting to their left.
3. Questioner returns and asks each player a question in turn.
4. After one full round, questioner states their theory.
5. Group responds: Warm (close) or Cold (not close).
6. Game ends when the questioner correctly identifies the rule.

**Variations**

- **Imposter** — Before the questioner returns, one group member secretly receives a different hidden rule (decided by a simple chit draw from a bag). That person is the imposter. The questioner must identify both the group's rule and the imposter.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Unlimited rounds — questioner can ask as many rounds as they need to identify the rule.
- **Medium:** Limited rounds — questioner has a set number of rounds to identify the rule.
- **Hard:** Imposter variation — one group member secretly has a different hidden rule. Questioner must identify both the group's rule and the imposter.

**Materials**

- Simple chits (for imposter variation — two types: Rule and Imposter)

**reference prompts — hidden rules the group can agree on**

1. Use a colour word in every answer.
2. Include a number in every answer.
3. Start every answer with "I think."
4. End every answer with the same word.
5. Speak only in questions.
6. Give opposite answers.
7. Use only 3 words per answer.
8. Add an animal name in every answer.
9. Never use the word "I."
10. Always say "yes" before answering.
11. Include a food item in every answer.
12. Act and speak — add one action before answering.
13. Give only very short answers.
14. Give only long or detailed answers.
15. Stretch one word in every answer.
16. Smile while answering.
17. Speak like a robot.
18. Speak in a whisper.
19. Answer like a baby.

### voice toss

_Roll Call · physical game · card: Voice_

> Before you throw the ball, call a volume. The catcher has to say any word or sentence at exactly that volume.

**How to play**

1. Children stand or sit in a circle.
2. Before throwing a soft ball, the thrower calls one of three volume levels.

**Materials**

- Soft ball

### what's that sound

_Roll Call · facilitated · card: Ears_

> Pick a card. Don't show anyone. Make the sound of the ticked object — the rest of us guess.

### tale trail

_Playground · physical game_

> Shuffle Story Cards and place face-up in 4 piles (character, object, event, emotion). Pick one card from any pile. Children place it on the story mat and each child continues the story line through the cards they pick and place.

### watch your step

_Playground · physical game_

> Speak on your topic for a set time to move a set number of steps. Hesitate or stop, and the timer stops — you move by however many steps the time board gives you.

**How to play**

1. Pieces start at the bottom of the gameboard.
2. The time board at the side maps speaking time to steps — e.g. 15 seconds = 1 step, 30s = 2, 45s = 3, 60s = 4, and so on.
3. Players can see the board so they can plan how long they want to keep speaking.
4. Teacher reads out a prompt from the digital deck in the app.
5. Player starts speaking on that prompt.
6. The timer runs.
7. Hesitation, repetition, or going off-topic stops the timer — the piece moves by the number of steps the time board allows for that clock reading.
8. Landing on a Slippery Step moves the piece back.
9. Landing on a Risk Step: player attempts a Risk Card challenge — completing it earns a Reward Card.
10. First individual / team to the top of the board wins.

**Variations**

- **Individual play** — Each player plays with their own piece. Their turn, their topic, their time.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Timer visible to the speaker.
- **Medium:** Timer not visible to the speaker.
- **Hard:** Use of 3-word incorporation — the opposing player gives the speaker 3 words to incorporate into their speech before the turn begins.

**Materials**

- Gameboard
- Time board (speaking-time to steps reference)
- Player pieces
- Risk Cards
- Reward Cards
- Digital prompts — https://wordwall.net/resource/84114197

### psychiatrist

_Playground · facilitated_

> Questioner steps outside. Group agrees on a hidden rule. Questioner returns and asks questions to figure out the rule.

**How to play**

1. Questioner steps outside or turns away.
2. Group secretly agrees on a hidden rule governing all their answers — for example, everyone ends every answer with the same word, or everyone answers as if they are the person sitting to their left.
3. Questioner returns and asks each player a question in turn.
4. After one full round, questioner states their theory.
5. Group responds: Warm (close) or Cold (not close).
6. Game ends when the questioner correctly identifies the rule.
7. Post-reveal: group identifies the single most useful question asked and explains why.
8. At 8–12, the group may secretly change the rule halfway through.

**Variations**

- **Imposter** — Before the questioner returns, one group member secretly receives a different hidden rule (decided by a simple chit draw from a bag). That person is the imposter. The questioner must identify both the group's rule and the imposter.

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Unlimited rounds — questioner can ask as many rounds as they need to identify the rule.
- **Medium:** Limited rounds — questioner has a set number of rounds to identify the rule.
- **Hard:** Imposter variation — one group member secretly has a different hidden rule. Questioner must identify both the group's rule and the imposter.

**Materials**

- Simple chits (for imposter variation — two types: Rule and Imposter)

7. Use only 3 words per answer.
8. Add an animal name in every answer.
9. Never use the word "I."
10. Always say "yes" before answering.
11. Include a food item in every answer.
12. Act and speak — add one action before answering.
13. Give only very short answers.
14. Give only long or detailed answers.
15. Stretch one word in every answer.
16. Smile while answering.
17. Speak like a robot.
18. Speak in a whisper.
19. Answer like a baby.

### Experiment segment

### l1 levers e1 — longer lever, less effort

_Experiment · physical game · card: L1 Levers e1_

> same load, different lever lengths. does it get easier to lift when the lever is longer?

**How to play**

1. Set up a simple lever across a T-connector fulcrum.
2. Hang a fixed load on one side and pull down on the other using a spring scale — first with a short arm (20cm), then a medium arm (30cm), then a long arm (40cm).
3. Each child takes at least one reading.
4. Record every reading in the result table.
5. First use: children discover the pattern.
6. Second use: children predict before reading — "I think the 40cm will need less force because last time it was the smallest number."

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Teacher reads the card. Child hangs load, pulls the spring scale and reads the number. Teacher writes.
- **Medium:** Child reads the card step by step, does the setup, and writes their own reading in the result table.
- **Hard:** Child predicts before each reading — "this one will be smaller/bigger because…" — and checks whether the prediction was right.

**Materials**

- Experiment Card: L1 Levers e1 (laminated, wipe-clean)
- PVC pipe cut to 20cm, 30cm, 40cm — 1 of each per group
- T-connector — 1 per group, used as fulcrum
- Spring scale 2kg — 1 per group

- Measuring tape, erasable marker

### l1 levers e3 — equal weights balance

_Experiment · physical game · card: L1 Levers e3_

**How to play**

### l1 levers e4 — any equal weight balances

_Experiment · physical game · card: L1 Levers e4_

**How to play**

### l1 pulleys e3 — pulley height does not change effort

_Experiment · physical game · card: L1 Pulleys e3_

**Difficulty**

- **Easy:** Child measures both scenarios.
- **Medium:** Child predicts whether the readings will match before measuring.
- **Hard:** Child explains why they match — the rope carries the same tension on both sides.
